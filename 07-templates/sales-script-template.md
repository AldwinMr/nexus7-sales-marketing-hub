## Goal of the Conversation

Understand the business owner’s current sales and inventory process, identify pain points, and invite them to a Nexus7 demo.

---

## 1. Opening Line

Hi [Name], I’m reaching out because Nexus7BusinessOS helps small businesses like pharmacies, groceries, hardware stores, and cafes manage sales, inventory, customers, and reports in one practical system.

---

## 2. Context Setter

The reason I wanted to connect is that many small business owners are still using notebooks, Excel, or basic POS tools that do not give them a clear view of inventory, daily sales, and customer activity.

Nexus7 is designed to make those daily workflows easier.

---

## 3. Qualification Questions

1. How are you currently tracking sales?
2. Do you use a POS system today?
3. How do you monitor inventory or stock levels?
4. How do you check your daily sales total?
5. Do you manage customer records or repeat buyers?
6. What part of your current process takes the most time?
7. Are you using Excel, notebooks, a basic POS, or another system?
8. Who usually handles sales and inventory updates?

## 8. Common Objections and Responses

### Objection: “We already use Excel.”

Response:

That makes sense. A lot of small businesses start with Excel. Nexus7 is useful when Excel becomes harder to manage because sales, inventory, customers, and reports need to be connected.

Follow-up question:

What part of your Excel process takes the most time today?

---

### Objection: “We already have a POS.”

Response:

That’s good. The question is whether your current POS gives you the workflow and reports you need. Nexus7 can be compared against your current setup, especially around business-type-specific screens, onboarding templates, and reporting.

Follow-up question:

What do you wish your current POS handled better?

---

### Objection: “It might be too expensive.”

Response:

That’s fair. The goal is to keep Nexus7 practical for SMEs. The demo can help you decide whether the time saved, cleaner tracking, and better visibility are worth the monthly cost.

Follow-up question:

What would make a system worth paying for in your business?

---

### Objection: “We are not ready yet.”

Response:

No problem. The demo is still useful because it can help you see what to prepare before switching to a better system.

Follow-up question:

What would need to happen before you feel ready?

---

### Objection: “My staff may not know how to use it.”

Response:

That is a valid concern. Nexus7 is being designed around simple business workflows, not complicated enterprise screens. The goal is to make the daily sales process easy enough for staff to learn quickly.

Follow-up question:

Who on your team would be using the system every day?

---

### Objection: “We do not want a complicated setup.”

Response:

That is exactly why Nexus7 uses business-type templates. The goal is to reduce setup friction by starting with workflows and product structures that already match your type of business.

Follow-up question:

What part of setup worries you most — products, staff training, inventory, or reports?
