## What the Customer May Really Mean

Explain the underlying concern.

Example:

The customer may not yet understand the business value, may be comparing Nexus7 to a cheaper basic POS, or may be worried about adding another monthly cost.

---

## Recommended Response

That’s fair. The goal of Nexus7 is not just to replace a cash register. It helps connect sales, inventory, customers, and reports so you can run the business with better daily visibility.

The demo can help you decide whether the time saved and cleaner tracking are worth the cost.

---

## Do Not Say

- “This is the cheapest option.”
- “This will solve all your problems.”
- “You will instantly increase sales.”
- “Our system is better than everyone.”
- “You do not need training.”
- “Implementation is always easy.”

## Example Objections

### “We already use Excel.”

Recommended response:

That makes sense. A lot of small businesses start with Excel. Nexus7 becomes useful when Excel becomes harder to manage because sales, inventory, customers, and reports need to be connected.

Follow-up question:

What part of your Excel process takes the most time today?

---

### “We already have a POS.”

Recommended response:

That’s good. The question is whether your current POS gives you the workflow and reports you need. Nexus7 can be compared against your current setup, especially around business-type-specific screens, onboarding templates, and reporting.

Follow-up question:

What do you wish your current POS handled better?

---

### “My staff may not know how to use it.”

Recommended response:

That is a valid concern. Nexus7 is designed around simple daily business workflows. The goal is to make the sales process easy enough for staff to learn through guided onboarding and practical screens.

Follow-up question:

Who on your team would use the system every day?

---

### “We are not ready yet.”

Recommended response:

No problem. A demo is still useful because it can help you see what to prepare before switching to a better system.

Follow-up question:

What would need to happen before you feel ready?
